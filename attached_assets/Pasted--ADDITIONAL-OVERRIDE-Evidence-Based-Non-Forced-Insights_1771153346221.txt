### C) Teacher accountability boundaries (CRITICAL)
The teacher MUST NOT be held accountable for:
- number of topics covered
- breadth of curriculum content
- what content is taught (this is curriculum-driven)

Teacher IS accountable for:
- clarity of explanation
- engagement behaviors (prompts, checks for understanding)
- responsiveness to confusion
- motivational/encouraging communication
- facilitating student participation during activities

**All insights must be framed around delivery and interaction, not content scope.**

### D) Replace time-only activity transcript analysis with meaningful signals
For each activity (and for combined Section Checks), generate at most:
- 0‚Äì3 "What Worked"
- 0‚Äì3 "Could Improve"

Signals you MAY use (non-time-based):
1) Engagement prompts: teacher asks students to respond, explain, vote, or type in chat (evidence: transcript excerpt).
2) Comprehension checks: teacher asks "did you understand", asks for reasoning, asks for steps (evidence: transcript excerpt).
3) Responsiveness: teacher addresses confusion or questions seen in chat (evidence: chat + transcript proximity).
4) Explanation correctness/consistency (delivery-focused): detect contradictions, unclear steps, or mismatched instructions vs the question intent.
   - Only flag if there is strong transcript evidence (e.g., conflicting stated rule/step or self-correction).
5) Student participation quality: many skipped answers, low completion, or no chat responses after prompts (data signal).
6) Reinforcement quality: praise/encouragement used appropriately during struggle moments (evidence: transcript excerpt).

Timing overlap (teacher speaking during activity) should be treated as a *context detail*, not an automatic negative. Only flag if it likely interfered (e.g., students asking confusion questions, high skip rate, repeated wrong answers during the overlap).
